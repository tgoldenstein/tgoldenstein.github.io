Hi there! My name is Taylor Goldenstein. I'm currently a general assignment reporter at the Los Angeles Times, after having just finished a year-long stint on the Data Desk.


I graduated in December 2013 with a journalism degree from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. Before joining the Times, I was a 2014 visiting fellow at the Nieman Foundation for Journalism at Harvard. I've previously interned at the Chicago Tribune and Tampa Bay Times among other publications.


One day I'd like to specialize in investigative reporting, thanks to a rewarding story I worked on about a fraudulent rescue squad that led to action by the state, resignations and an arrest (more on that here), my former professor Brant Houston and, of course, All The President's Men.
